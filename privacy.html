Privacy Policy

How Lead Community Services handles personal information.

Lead Community Services respects participant privacy and handles personal information with care, confidentiality, and consent.

Business Details

Lead Community Services operates under ABN 27 621 378 443 as an NDIS provider for self-managed and plan-managed participants.

Contact: Leadcommunitys@gmail.com or 0425 830 061.

Information We Collect

We may collect your name, contact details, participant details, NDIS plan information, support needs, goals, service preferences, consent records, and information needed to deliver safe and appropriate supports.

Why We Collect It

We collect information to respond to enquiries, assess service fit, prepare service agreements, provide supports, communicate with participants or nominees, meet record-keeping obligations, manage complaints, and support participant safety.

Consent and Sharing

We only share personal information with consent, where it is needed to deliver agreed supports, or where required by law. This may include communication with a participant, nominee, support coordinator, plan manager, or relevant service provider.

Website Forms

Website enquiries are sent to Leadcommunitys@gmail.com. The online form may be processed by a website form provider before the enquiry reaches us. Do not use the form for emergencies, crisis support, or highly sensitive information that is not needed for the initial enquiry.

Storage and Security

We take reasonable steps to keep personal information secure and limit access to people who need it for service delivery, administration, complaints handling, or legal obligations.

Access, Correction, and Deletion

You can ask to access, correct, or delete personal information held by Lead Community Services. Some records may need to be kept where required for service, legal, insurance, or complaint-handling reasons.

Complaints About Privacy

Privacy concerns can be raised by email or phone. We aim to acknowledge privacy complaints within 2 business days and respond fairly. If the concern relates to NDIS-funded supports, you may also contact the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission.
