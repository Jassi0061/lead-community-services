Safeguards

Clear, transparent practice for safer support.

Unregistered providers still need to work safely, honestly, and competently. Lead Community Services uses practical safeguards so participants know what to expect before support begins.

NDIS Code of Conduct

Services are delivered with respect, care, skill, integrity, and transparency, with support boundaries explained before commencement.

Service Agreements

Participants receive clear information about supports, pricing, cancellation terms, communication, and how changes are handled.

Privacy and Consent

Personal information is collected only for support delivery, handled confidentially, and shared only with consent or where required by law.

Complaints and Feedback

Concerns are acknowledged within 2 business days, reviewed fairly, and can be escalated to the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission.

Business and Qualifications

Lead Community Services operates under ABN 27 621 378 443. Counselling is delivered by a qualified Counsellor, and capacity-building support is informed by qualified training and assessment experience.

Pricing and Cancellations

Pricing, travel, cancellation terms, reports, and service boundaries are confirmed in a written service agreement before supports begin. NDIS pricing rules and limits are followed where applicable.

Screening and Insurance

Relevant worker screening, police checks, Working With Children Check, First Aid/CPR, public liability, and professional indemnity details can be provided before services commence where applicable.

Service Boundaries

Therapy-related support is limited to coordination and capacity-building unless delivered by an appropriately qualified allied health professional. This service is not a crisis or emergency response service.

Questions

Helpful details before you refer.

Are you a registered NDIS provider?

Lead Community Services is an unregistered NDIS provider. We can work with self-managed and plan-managed participants where the requested support matches the participant's plan, funding, and current NDIS requirements.

Can you support NDIA-managed participants?

As an unregistered provider, Lead Community Services is positioned for self-managed and plan-managed participants. NDIA-managed participants generally need to use registered providers.

Do you offer counselling?

Yes. Counselling services are available and delivered by a qualified Counsellor, with support shaped around participant wellbeing and personal goals.

Do you provide therapy or crisis support?

Therapy-related services are limited to coordination and capacity-building unless delivered by an appropriately qualified allied health professional. Lead Community Services is not an emergency or crisis response service. In an emergency, call 000.

Can services start quickly?

Often, yes. Start dates depend on the support type, location, qualifications required, service agreement, consent, and any required risk or health documentation.

How can I make a complaint?

You can raise concerns directly with Lead Community Services by phone or email. Complaints are acknowledged within 2 business days, reviewed fairly, and handled without retaliation or changes to support because a concern was raised. You can also contact the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission if the concern relates to NDIS-funded supports.

Which areas do you cover?

Lead Community Services is positioned for Brisbane, Logan, Ipswich, Moreton Bay, and nearby South East Queensland communities.
